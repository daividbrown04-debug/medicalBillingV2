End-to-End Process

We handle the auth. You book the case.

Most practices spend 14+ clinical and admin hours per week on prior authorizations. We absorb that burden entirely — from the first submission to the peer-to-peer call your provider shouldn't have to arrange.

Request received

Referral or order lands in our queue — by fax, EHR task, or intake portal. <2 hr acknowledgment.

Clinical build + submission

Full auth package built and submitted same business day with all clinical justification attached.

Daily payer follow-up

Daily status calls to the payer. Escalated internally if any payer exceeds their own stated turnaround SLA.

Peer-to-peer arranged

If denied, we arrange the peer-to-peer call with the clinical case pre-built. Providers spend under 8 minutes.

Full Service Scope

Everything that stands between your patient and their care.

Most time-consuming

Peer-to-peer escalation

When a payer denies on clinical grounds, the fastest path to reversal is a physician-to-physician call. We arrange it, prep the clinical case, and put only the 8-minute call on your physician's calendar — not the 2 hours of prep work.

  • We arrange time with payer medical director
  • Pre-built clinical argument using latest CPG evidence
  • Provider only needs to attend the call — not build the case
  • Same-day follow-up to confirm reversal in writing

Expiration tracking & re-auth

Every approved auth is logged with its expiration date, procedure window, and units allowed. We send alerts 10 and 3 days before expiry and can re-auth automatically so no case gets scheduled with a lapsed authorization.

Urgent / same-day processing

Emergency or urgent auth requests flagged to our priority queue. Average urgent turnaround: 4.2 hours.

Retro-authorization recovery

Missed the auth window? We handle retroactive authorization requests with full clinical documentation — 71% retro approval rate across specialties.

Real-time status updates

Log into your ClearView™ dashboard to see every auth request — pending, approved, denied, or in peer-to-peer. Or receive a text alert when approval lands.

The Cost of Delayed Auth

For every week an auth sits pending, you lose more than one claim.

The real cost of a slow auth process is never just the denied claim. It's the rescheduled procedure, the frustrated patient who goes elsewhere, the physician time spent on hold, and the A/R that balloons into months of outstanding balances.

  • Average practice loses 14+ admin hours/week on auth tasks
  • Delayed auths cause 1 in 4 procedure cancellations (MGMA 2024)
  • 9% of patients abandon care when auth delay exceeds 5 days
  • Each rescheduled case = lost slot, lost co-pay, re-booking cost
