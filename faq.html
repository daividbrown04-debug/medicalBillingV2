Switching billing companies

For most practices, the full transition takes 2–4 weeks. That timeline covers EHR access setup, payer remittance address updates, clearinghouse re-routing, and an audit of your existing A/R so nothing falls through the gap. We submit your first claims before your old company submits their last — no interruption in revenue flow.

Credentialing re-enrollment with payers is handled under your existing group NPI, so there's no interruption in payment during the transition. Payer contracts stay with the group, not with your biller.

Your existing A/R belongs to your practice — not your old biller. We conduct a free A/R audit during onboarding and take over outstanding claims as part of our standard transition. Anything in aged A/R (90+ days) that needs special recovery attention is handled under our contingency A/R recovery model at no upfront cost.

No. You stay in your existing EHR. We work inside your practice management system using native workflows — your staff keeps doing exactly what they're doing today. The only change they notice is that claim submission is handled by us, and the dashboard they get to track everything is ClearView™, which takes about 20 minutes to learn.

We can help you review your current contract's termination terms. Most billing agreements allow 30–90 day notice periods. We'll start the transition process on our end so you can begin on day one of your new contract start date, not weeks after. We'll also help you draft the termination notice if you need language review.

Pricing & contracts

Full RCM is billed as a percentage of net collections — meaning you only pay us when you get paid. We start at 3.9% and adjust for specialty complexity and monthly volume. Credentialing is $325 flat per provider per payer. A/R recovery is 12–18% contingency only.

There are no setup fees, no per-claim fees, no report fees, no exit fees, and no termination penalties. If you want to see the full fee schedule, request a quote and we'll send a written breakdown in 24 hours.

Flat-fee billing companies make the same money whether your collection rate is 80% or 96%. Percentage-based billing aligns our incentives with yours — we earn more only when you collect more. It also means our fee adjusts automatically when your volume changes: if you add a provider, we earn more; if you reduce hours, you pay less.

If we don't hit that benchmark, we reduce our fee to 0% until we do. The specific numbers are written into your service agreement before we start. This guarantee is binding, not marketing language.

Denials & appeals

Every denial is appealed — 100%, no exceptions. Our DenialShield™ system categorizes each denial reason, assigns it to the correct appeal track (clinical, coding, administrative, or eligibility), builds the appeal package, and tracks it to resolution. You see every denial and its current status in your ClearView™ dashboard in real time.

We also produce monthly root-cause denial reports that identify patterns — so upstream issues (like a coding habit or a payer policy change) get fixed before they generate more denials.

Credentialing

Payer credentialing timelines are set by payers, not by us — and they range from 30 days (some regional Medicaid plans) to 180 days (some commercial payers). The national average is 45–90 days. We track every application weekly and escalate when payers miss their own stated timelines.

Meanwhile, we can often arrange provisional billing rights (shadow billing or "held" claims to release upon enrollment) so your new provider generates billable services from day one — the payment just releases when enrollment is confirmed.

CAQH ProView is the centralized credentialing database used by most commercial payers. We set up and maintain your providers' CAQH profiles, complete the quarterly re-attestation, and keep documents current so enrollment applications don't get stalled on missing info.
