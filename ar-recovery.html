What makes a claim recoverable?

  • Timely filing window is still open. Most payers allow 365 days; Medicare allows 12 months from date of service. Some states mandate 24 months.
  • The denial reason is appealable. CO-16 (missing info), CO-97 (not authorized), CO-4 (coding) — all correctable.
  • The clinical documentation exists. If the record supports the service, we can build the appeal. If documentation is missing, we'll tell you upfront.
  • The patient balance is valid. If insurance has been exhausted, we can re-bill patients with a compliant statement — never aggressive collections.
  • We do not use third-party debt collectors — ever.

Common A/R We Recover

The accounts most billers give up on. We don't.

Timely filing appeals

We request proof of timely filing from your clearinghouse and submit appeals with documented evidence of original transmission. We recover 58% of timely-filing denials.

Credentialing-related denials

Claims denied because the provider wasn't yet enrolled — or was enrolled but not linked to the group. We reconstruct the credentialing timeline and appeal on effective-date grounds.

Stale prior auth denials

Services rendered under an auth that expired before the claim was adjudicated. We request retro-auth or submit medical necessity appeals with the original authorization reference.

Coordination of benefits issues

When primary and secondary payers disagree on responsibility, claims often fall through the cracks. We identify COB errors, correct sequencing, and re-bill both payers correctly.

Downcoded / bundled claims

Payer bundled your codes or downcoded your E&M level. We unbundle with documentation support or appeal the E&M downcode with a clinical audit trail from your notes.

Non-covered services with ABN

If an ABN was obtained but never billed to the patient, we generate compliant patient statements and collect the balance — compliantly and without damaging the patient relationship.
